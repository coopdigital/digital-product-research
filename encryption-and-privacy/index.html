[encryption-and-privacy]

  • We believe people should understand and have control over the technology they use
  • We believe technology should work for people and communities, not against them

To many people, privacy and data security are abstract concepts.

Yet when personal details are leaked or secure systems breached, the outcome is all too real.

Can we get people to trust Co-op with highly personal information? And could we build something that would actually protect their privacy?

We built Vault (later renamed Paperfree), an app that allowed people to store scans of important documents as encrypted files on their phone - not in the cloud. We recruited X early users, helped them install the app on their phone, and sent them away to use it as they saw fit.

Our testers trusted the product to be secure, and understood our attempts to explain that their files were encrypted - that we would not be able to view them, even if we wanted to. However we saw numbers of active users of the app drop off, as it was not clear to our users what the app was for and how it would benefit them (beyond being an organisational tool).
